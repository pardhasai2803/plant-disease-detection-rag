DISEASE: Cherry_(including_sour)___Powdery_mildew
CROP: Cherry (including sour)
TYPE: Fungal
SYMPTOMS: Powdery mildew on cherry trees is characterized by a white, powdery coating on the leaves, stems, and fruits. The symptoms typically start as small, circular patches on the upper surface of the leaves, which can eventually cover the entire leaf. Infected leaves may become distorted, turn yellow, and drop prematurely. The disease can also cause a reduction in fruit quality and yield. In severe cases, the powdery mildew can infect the fruit, causing a white, powdery coating to form on the skin, which can lead to premature ripening and spoilage.
CAUSES: Powdery mildew on cherry trees is caused by the fungal pathogen Podosphaera clandestina. The fungus overwinters on infected plant debris and can be spread by wind, water, and insects.
FAVORABLE_CONDITIONS: The disease thrives in cool, humid environments with temperatures between 15°C and 25°C (59°F and 77°F). High humidity, particularly during the night, and moderate temperatures during the day create ideal conditions for the disease to spread. Overcrowding, poor air circulation, and excessive nitrogen fertilization can also contribute to the development of powdery mildew.
CHEMICAL_TREATMENT: Chemical treatments for powdery mildew on cherry trees include fungicides such as sulfur, copper, and triadimenol. These fungicides can be applied as a preventative measure or at the first sign of infection. It is essential to follow the recommended application rates and timing to avoid damaging the trees or contaminating the fruit.
ORGANIC_TREATMENT: Organic treatments for powdery mildew on cherry trees include neem oil, horticultural oil, and potassium bicarbonate. These treatments can be used as a preventative measure or to control the disease after it has developed. Additionally, removing infected plant debris, improving air circulation, and avoiding excessive nitrogen fertilization can help to reduce the spread of the disease.
PREVENTION: To prevent powdery mildew on cherry trees, it is essential to maintain good tree hygiene, including removing infected plant debris and pruning infected branches. Improving air circulation by thinning the trees and removing weeds can also help to reduce the spread of the disease. Applying a fungicide or organic treatment as a preventative measure can also help to protect the trees from infection.
SEVERITY: Medium to High
ADDITIONAL_INFO: Regular monitoring of the trees for signs of powdery mildew is crucial for early detection and treatment. Cherry trees that are susceptible to powdery mildew should be planted in well-ventilated areas, and irrigation should be managed to avoid excessive moisture on the leaves and fruits. The use of resistant cultivars can also help to reduce the risk of powdery mildew infection.
