DISEASE: Strawberry___Leaf_scorch
CROP: Strawberry
TYPE: Fungal
SYMPTOMS: Strawberry leaf scorch is a fungal disease that causes symptoms such as scorched or burnt-looking leaves, often with a reddish-brown or purple discoloration. The leaves may become brittle and crumbly, and in severe cases, the plant's growth may be stunted. The disease typically starts on the older leaves and progresses to the younger leaves. Infected leaves may also develop small, dark spots or lesions.
CAUSES: Strawberry leaf scorch is caused by the fungal pathogen Diplocarpon earlianum. The fungus overwinters on infected plant debris and can be spread through rain splash, irrigation, or contaminated tools and equipment.
FAVORABLE_CONDITIONS: The disease thrives in cool and wet conditions, with temperatures between 60°F and 75°F (15°C and 24°C) and high humidity. Poor air circulation, overcrowding, and excessive nitrogen fertilization can also contribute to the development of the disease.
CHEMICAL_TREATMENT: Chemical treatments for strawberry leaf scorch include fungicides such as chlorothalonil, copper-based products, and systemic fungicides like myclobutanil. These products should be applied according to the label instructions and repeated as necessary to control the disease.
ORGANIC_TREATMENT: Organic treatments for strawberry leaf scorch include removing and disposing of infected leaves, improving air circulation, and reducing nitrogen fertilization. Copper-based organic fungicides, neem oil, and sulfur-based products can also be used to control the disease. Additionally, introducing beneficial microorganisms like Trichoderma harzianum can help to suppress the fungal pathogen.
PREVENTION: Prevention measures for strawberry leaf scorch include removing weeds and debris, improving air circulation, and avoiding excessive nitrogen fertilization. Planting resistant strawberry varieties, using clean planting material, and practicing good sanitation can also help to prevent the disease. Regular monitoring for symptoms and removing infected leaves can help to prevent the spread of the disease.
SEVERITY: Medium
ADDITIONAL_INFO: Strawberry leaf scorch can be managed through a combination of cultural, chemical, and organic controls. It's essential to monitor the plants regularly and take action at the first sign of symptoms to prevent the disease from spreading. In addition to controlling the disease, providing optimal growing conditions, including full sun, well-draining soil, and adequate water, can help to promote healthy plant growth and reduce the risk of infection.
