DISEASE: Corn_(maize)___Cercospora_leaf_spot Gray_leaf_spot
CROP: Maize (Corn)
TYPE: Fungal
SYMPTOMS: The symptoms of Cercospora leaf spot, also known as Gray leaf spot, include elongated, rectangular lesions that are typically 1-3 inches long and 0.2-0.5 inches wide. These lesions are usually gray or tan in color with a distinctive dark brown or black border. The lesions can merge to form larger areas of dead tissue, leading to blighting of the leaves. In severe cases, the disease can progress to the upper leaves, causing significant yield loss. Infected plants may also exhibit premature senescence, where the leaves turn yellow and die prematurely.
CAUSES: Cercospora leaf spot is caused by the fungus Cercospora zeae-maydis. The fungus overwinters on infected corn debris and in the soil, producing spores that are dispersed by wind and water. The disease is favored by warm temperatures, high humidity, and prolonged periods of moisture.
FAVORABLE_CONDITIONS: The disease thrives in temperatures between 64°F and 90°F (18°C and 32°C), with optimal growth occurring at 77°F (25°C). High humidity, prolonged moisture, and excessive nitrogen fertilization also favor disease development. Additionally, corn planted in low-lying areas or with poor air circulation is more susceptible to the disease.
CHEMICAL_TREATMENT: Chemical treatments for Cercospora leaf spot include the application of fungicides such as azoxystrobin, pyraclostrobin, and trifloxystrobin. These fungicides can be applied at the early stages of disease development, typically when lesions are first visible. Repeat applications may be necessary to maintain control.
ORGANIC_TREATMENT: Organic treatments for Cercospora leaf spot include the use of copper-based fungicides, neem oil, and biological control agents such as Trichoderma harzianum. Crop rotation, sanitation, and the use of resistant corn varieties can also help manage the disease.
PREVENTION: Prevention measures for Cercospora leaf spot include crop rotation, removing infected corn debris, and using resistant corn varieties. Planting corn in well-draining soil with adequate air circulation can also help reduce disease incidence. Additionally, avoiding excessive nitrogen fertilization and maintaining a balanced fertilization program can help prevent the disease.
SEVERITY: High
ADDITIONAL_INFO: Cercospora leaf spot is a significant disease of corn worldwide, causing significant yield losses and reducing grain quality. The disease can be managed through a combination of cultural, chemical, and biological control methods. Resistant corn varieties are available, and breeding programs are ongoing to develop new, high-yielding varieties with improved disease resistance. Accurate diagnosis and timely management are critical to preventing significant yield losses.
